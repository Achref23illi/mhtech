Common Washing Machine Problems

Washing machines can experience a variety of issues that affect their performance. Our technicians are trained to diagnose and repair all common washing machine problems including:

Washing Machine Won't Start

If your washing machine refuses to start, it can be due to a variety of reasons, such as a faulty power outlet, a malfunctioning control board, or issues with the door switch. Our technicians can quickly diagnose and fix the problem, getting your machine back in action.

Water Leakage

Water leaks can cause significant damage to your home. Leaks can originate from a damaged door seal, loose hose connections, or a cracked drum. Our team will thoroughly inspect your machine to identify and repair the source of the leak.

Drainage Problems

If your washing machine isn't draining properly, it could be due to a clogged pump, a blocked hose, or issues with the drainage system. We will ensure that water flows freely, preventing any potential water damage.

Failure To Spin

A washing machine that doesn't spin can leave your clothes soaking wet. This issue might be due to a broken drive belt, a faulty lid switch, or problems with the motor. Our experts can swiftly address these issues to restore your machine's spinning function.

Vibrations And Shaking

Excessive vibrations or shaking during operation can be caused by an unbalanced load, worn-out suspension springs, or other mechanical issues. We will stabilize your machine for smooth and quiet operation.

Error Codes

Modern washing machines often display error codes when something goes wrong. Our technicians are well-versed in interpreting these codes and diagnosing the underlying issues.

Excessive Noise

Unusual noises during a wash cycle can be alarming. These sounds often indicate problems with the drum bearings, motor, or other internal components. We will pinpoint the cause and provide a lasting solution.

Control Panel Issues

Malfunctioning control panels can prevent you from selecting the right wash cycles or settings. Whether it's a digital display, knobs, buttons, or dial display, we have the expertise to fix it.

Spots On Clothes

Persistent spots on clothes that don't respond to soap and water may be caused by transmission oil from a malfunctioning transmission.

Washing Machine Maintenance Tips

Proper maintenance can extend the life of your washing machine and prevent common issues. Here are some helpful tips to keep your washing machine running efficiently:

Don't Overload

Resist the temptation to overload your washing machine. Overloading can strain the motor, damage the drum, and lead to ineffective cleaning.

Clean the Drum Regularly

Run an empty hot water cycle with a cup of white vinegar or washing machine cleaner monthly to remove soap residue and prevent odors.

Check Hoses

Inspect water inlet and drain hoses for cracks, bulges, or leaks every 6 months. Replace damaged hoses promptly to prevent water damage.

Clean the Lint Filter

If your machine has a lint filter, clean it regularly to prevent clogs and ensure proper water drainage.

Leave the Door Open

After each use, leave the door or lid slightly open to allow air circulation and prevent mold and mildew growth.

Use the Right Detergent

Always use the appropriate detergent for your machine type (high-efficiency or standard) to prevent excessive suds and potential damage.

Frequently Asked Questions About Washing Machine Repair

Here, we've compiled answers to common questions about washing machine repairs, maintenance, and our service process. If you don't find the information you're looking for, please contact us directly.

How much does washing machine repair cost?

The cost of washing machine repair varies depending on the specific issue, the parts required, and the complexity of the repair. Our standard diagnostic fee is $85, which is waived if you proceed with our repair service. After diagnosing the problem, we provide an upfront estimate including parts and labor costs, so you know exactly what to expect before we begin any repairs.

How long does a washing machine repair take?

Most washing machine repairs can be completed within 1-2 hours once our technician arrives. Simple issues like replacing a belt or fixing a switch may take less time, while more complex problems such as motor or transmission repairs might require longer. In most cases, we can complete the repair in a single visit, especially if we have the necessary parts on hand.

Can I prevent washing machine breakdowns?

Yes, regular maintenance can significantly reduce the risk of washing machine breakdowns. Some preventive measures include: not overloading the machine, cleaning the drum regularly, checking and cleaning filters, inspecting hoses periodically, using the right type and amount of detergent, and leaving the door ajar after each use to prevent mold growth. Our technicians can provide specific maintenance advice for your washing machine model during a repair visit.

Is it safe to repair a washing machine myself?

While it might be tempting to attempt DIY repairs, washing machines involve water and electricity, which can be dangerous when combined. Furthermore, modern washing machines contain complex electronic components that require specialized knowledge and tools to repair properly. Incorrect repairs can lead to further damage, void warranties, or create safety hazards. We recommend professional repairs to ensure safety and effective resolution of the issue.

How can I book a washing machine repair service with VM Service Tech?

Booking a washing machine repair service with us is easy. You can call us directly at (438) 800-7676, fill out our online request form, or send us a text message. Our customer service team is available 7 days a week to schedule appointments. For your convenience, we offer flexible appointment times, including evenings and weekends, to accommodate your schedule.

How do I know if my washing machine needs professional repair or replacement?

Consider the following factors when deciding between repair and replacement: the age of your machine (if over 10 years old, replacement might be more cost-effective), the frequency of breakdowns, the cost of repair versus a new machine (if repair costs exceed 50% of a new machine's price, replacement is often better), and energy efficiency (newer models are typically more energy-efficient). Our technicians can provide honest recommendations based on their assessment of your washing machine's condition.

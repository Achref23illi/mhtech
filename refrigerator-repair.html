Common Refrigerator Problems

Refrigerators can experience a variety of issues that affect their performance. Our technicians are trained to diagnose and repair all common refrigerator problems including:

Refrigerator Not Cooling

Your refrigerator is running, but the temperature inside isn't cold enough to keep your food fresh. This issue could be due to a malfunctioning compressor, evaporator fan, or thermostat.

Excessive Frost Build-Up

If your freezer or refrigerator has excessive frost build-up, it could be a sign of a faulty defrost system or door seal. This can lead to inefficient cooling and increased energy consumption.

Leaking Water

Water pooling under or inside your refrigerator often indicates a blocked defrost drain, a damaged water line, or issues with the water inlet valve. Prompt repair is crucial to prevent water damage to your floors.

Strange Noises

Unusual sounds like buzzing, clicking, or humming may suggest problems with the condenser fan, evaporator fan, or compressor. These issues can affect the refrigerator's performance and should be checked by a professional.

Faulty Temperature Control

Inconsistent or incorrect temperatures can result from a malfunctioning thermostat, temperature control board, or sensor. Addressing this issue helps maintain the optimal environment for your food.

Broken Ice Maker

Ice makers can fail due to water supply issues, clogged filters, or mechanical failures. Our technicians can diagnose and repair all types of ice maker problems.

Refrigerator Maintenance Tips

Proper maintenance can extend the life of your refrigerator and prevent common issues. Here are some helpful tips to keep your refrigerator running efficiently:

Clean Condenser Coils

Vacuum the condenser coils at the back or bottom of your refrigerator every 6 months to remove dust and debris that can reduce efficiency.

Check Door Seals

Ensure the door gaskets are clean and sealing properly. Test by closing the door on a piece of paper – if it pulls out easily, the seal may need replacement.

Maintain Proper Temperature

Keep your refrigerator between 37-40°F (3-4°C) and your freezer at 0°F (-18°C) for optimal food preservation and energy efficiency.

Don't Overfill

Proper air circulation is essential for cooling. Avoid overcrowding your refrigerator to allow cold air to flow efficiently around food items.

Clean Interior Regularly

Wipe down the interior with a solution of baking soda and water to prevent odors and maintain cleanliness.

Replace Water Filters

If your refrigerator has a water dispenser or ice maker, replace the water filter according to the manufacturer's recommendations, typically every 6 months.

Frequently Asked Questions About Refrigerator Repair

Here, we've compiled answers to common questions about refrigerator repairs, maintenance, and our service process. If you don't find the information you're looking for, please contact us directly.

How long does a typical refrigerator repair take?

Most refrigerator repairs take between 1-2 hours to complete once our technician arrives. However, the exact time depends on the complexity of the issue and the availability of replacement parts. Simple repairs like replacing a door seal or fixing a thermostat can be done in under an hour, while more complex issues like compressor replacement might take longer.

Is it worth repairing an old refrigerator?

This depends on several factors including the age of your refrigerator, the cost of repairs, and the cost of a new unit. As a general rule, if your refrigerator is over 10-15 years old and the repair would cost more than 50% of the price of a new model, replacement might be more economical. Our technicians can provide honest advice about whether repair or replacement makes more sense for your specific situation.

How quickly can you respond to emergency refrigerator repairs?

We understand that a non-functioning refrigerator can be an emergency. In most cases, we can offer same-day or next-day service for emergency refrigerator repairs, depending on technician availability and your location. We prioritize situations where food safety is at risk and do our best to accommodate urgent requests.

What are the signs that my refrigerator needs professional repair?

Common signs include: insufficient cooling despite being set to the correct temperature; excessive frost build-up; water leaking inside or outside the unit; unusual noises like clicking, buzzing, or rattling; the motor running constantly; food spoiling quickly; or the refrigerator cycling on and off too frequently. If you notice any of these issues, it's best to call for professional service before the problem worsens.

Do you repair built-in and commercial refrigerators?

Yes, our technicians are trained to repair a wide range of refrigerator types including standard refrigerators, side-by-side models, French door refrigerators, built-in units, and commercial refrigerators. We service all major brands and models, ensuring comprehensive refrigerator repair solutions for both residential and commercial clients.

What payment methods do you accept for refrigerator repair services?

We accept a variety of payment methods for your convenience, including major credit cards, debit cards, cash, and e-transfers. Our technicians carry payment terminals for on-the-spot payment processing. We also offer online payment options through our client portal for those who prefer digital transactions.

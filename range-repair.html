Common Electric Range Problems

Electric and induction ranges can experience various issues that affect their performance. Our technicians are trained to diagnose and repair all common range problems including:

Burner Not Heating

One of the most frequent issues is when a burner fails to heat up. This can be due to a faulty heating element, a defective switch, or a wiring problem within the range.

Inconsistent Temperature

If the oven's temperature fluctuates or doesn't match the set level, it might be due to a malfunctioning thermostat, a faulty temperature sensor, or a problem with the oven control board.

Range Display Issues

Problems with the control panel display, such as unresponsive buttons or incorrect readings, often stem from a malfunctioning control board or a wiring issue within the range.

Surface Element Won't Turn Off

If a surface element remains on even after turning it off, the problem is likely due to a malfunctioning surface element switch or a shorted-out wiring component.

Oven Not Heating

When the oven fails to heat, it could be caused by a defective bake or broil element, a faulty temperature sensor, or an issue with the oven's control system.

Oven Hinges Problem

Issues with oven hinges can cause the door to not close properly, leading to heat escaping and uneven cooking. This problem often arises due to worn-out or misaligned hinges and may require hinge replacement.

Induction Element Not Working

For induction ranges, a non-functioning element might be due to a faulty coil, sensor issues, or problems with the electronic control system specific to induction technology.

Electrical Problems

Various electrical issues can affect range performance, including power supply problems, circuit board failures, or wiring issues. Our technicians can safely identify and repair these electrical complications.

Self-Cleaning Function Issues

Problems with the self-cleaning function, such as failure to start or complete the cycle, often involve door lock mechanisms, temperature sensors, or control board malfunctions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here, we've compiled answers to common questions about electric and induction range repairs, maintenance, and our service process. If you don't find the information you're looking for, please contact us directly.

How do I know if my electric range needs repair?

Common signs that your electric range needs professional repair include: burners that don't heat properly or at all; uneven or inconsistent oven temperatures; control panel issues such as unresponsive buttons or display errors; the oven not heating up; burners staying on when turned off; unusual noises during operation; or a burning smell. If you experience any of these issues, it's advisable to call for professional service.

What should I do if my burner won't turn off?

If a burner on your electric range won't turn off, first turn off the range at the circuit breaker for safety. This situation typically indicates a problem with the burner's infinite switch or a short in the wiring. It's important to have this issue addressed promptly by a professional technician, as it could pose a fire hazard. Contact us for emergency service if you experience this problem.

Can I repair my electric range myself?

While some minor maintenance tasks can be performed safely by homeowners (such as cleaning surface elements or replacing oven light bulbs), most electric range repairs should be handled by qualified professionals. Ranges involve complex electrical systems and components that require specialized knowledge and tools to repair properly. DIY repairs can lead to further damage, void warranties, or create safety hazards. Our technicians are trained to diagnose and fix range issues safely and effectively.

How long does it take to repair an electric range?

Most standard electric range repairs can be completed within 1-2 hours once our technician arrives at your home. However, the exact timeframe depends on several factors, including the specific issue, the range model, and whether specialty parts are needed. Simple problems like replacing a surface element might take less than an hour, while more complex electrical issues could take longer. We aim to complete all repairs in a single visit when possible and will provide you with a time estimate before beginning work.

What's the difference between servicing standard electric and induction ranges?

Induction ranges differ from traditional electric ranges in that they use electromagnetic technology to heat cookware directly rather than using heating elements. This technology requires specialized diagnostic equipment and repair techniques. Our technicians are trained to service both types of ranges, but induction repairs often involve more complex electronic components and sensors. Rest assured that we have the expertise and tools to handle repairs for both standard electric and induction ranges efficiently.

How can I prepare for a range repair appointment?

To prepare for your range repair appointment: 1) Clear the area around your range to provide the technician with adequate workspace; 2) Make note of any specific issues you've noticed, including when they started and how frequently they occur; 3) Have your range's make and model information ready; 4) If applicable, write down any error codes displayed on the control panel; 5) Ensure someone over 18 years old is home during the appointment time; 6) For safety reasons, avoid using the range immediately before the appointment. These steps will help our technician diagnose and resolve the issue more efficiently.
